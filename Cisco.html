Computer Network Projects


During my sophomore and junior years of high school, I took computer networking classes called Cisco Networking Academy and Cisco Networking Professional (CCNP), which taught students how to establish, examine, and fix computer networks using Cisco routers, switches, and other proprietary devices.

The former class was designed to prepare students for the Cisco Certified Networking Associate (CCNA) examination. The latter was designed for delving deeper into concepts taught in the former. I have passed the CCNA and was certified to do the following:

  • Certified to configure routing protocols such as the Routing Information Protocol (RIP) and Open Shortest Path First (OSPF) that enhance communication between different networks.
  • Certified to design IPv4 & IPv6 networks and perform VLAN Trunking on Cisco switches and routers.The following projects are advanced network protocol simulations that I’ve done in CCNP.
